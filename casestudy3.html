Project Overview

The Product

Vehicle Ownership Transfer is a web design that allow users to transfer their Vehicle Ownership as well as can view their vehicle information.

Project information

  • Category: Web design
  • Project Duration: Feb 1 - June 25, 2023

PROBLEM

Transferring vehicle ownership is often a time-consuming and error-prone process involving multiple physical visits, manual paperwork, and lack of transparency. Current methods may result in delays, fraudulent activities, and lack of real-time status updates for both buyers and sellers. These inefficiencies hinder trust and create unnecessary burdens for individuals and transportation authorities.

THE GOAL

To develop a secure, transparent, and efficient digital platform that facilitates seamless vehicle ownership transfers.

ROLE

UX/UI Designer. [UI (user interface), UX (user experience)]

Responsibilities

User research, User persona, Problem statement, User journey map, Competitive audit, Ideation activitie, User flow, Storyboard, Low fidelity wireframe, Low fidelity prototypes, High fidelity mockups, High fidelity prototypes, Usability studies

UNDERSTANDING THE USER

User research: Summary

Conducted interviews and created empathy maps to understand the needs, behaviors, and motivations of vehicle buyers and sellers. The primary goal was to identify user frustrations with the current ownership transfer process. Most users were adults, often unfamiliar with the bureaucratic procedures and looking for a quicker, more transparent solution to transfer vehicle ownership without multiple physical visits or paperwork.

User Research: Pain Point

01

Time-Consuming Process

Users often face delays due to manual paperwork and long queues at government offices.

02

Lack of Transparency

Users are not informed of the transfer status and often feel left in the dark during the process.

03

Fraud Risk

There is a risk of fraudulent vehicle sales without verified documentation and ownership tracking.

DESIGN

Paper wireframe

I sketched paper wireframe of homepage, upload, login and signup page to explore various options for how to organize the information. I made notes on each screen of what I thought worked best, and incorporated those ideas into the digital low fidelity wireframes.



Digital wireframe

Next, I moved to Figma to create digital wireframes for all of the screens. Using a basic design system, I was able to create low fidelity wireframes that were still clean and organized. The homepage serves as a central hub where users can access a quick dashboard. The navigation gives users options to upload the vehicle information, transfer the ownership, use the search bar, and access the vehicle information.

Going Forward

Take away

This project is designed to simplify the vehicle ownership transfer process in Bhutan. It benefits individuals looking to buy or sell second-hand vehicles, as well as vehicle dealers. Through this project, I gained valuable experience in user-centered design, digital form workflows, and integrating data validation with user feedback for a seamless transfer experience.

Next Step

> Moving forward, I plan to implement features like digital document uploads, e-signature integration, and SMS/email notifications. I would also like to create an admin dashboard for RSTA officials to manage and verify ownership transfer requests more efficiently. For inclusivity, I aim to add voice assistance or screen reader support to make the platform more accessible.
